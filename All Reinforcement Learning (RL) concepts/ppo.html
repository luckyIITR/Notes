Proximal Policy Optimization (PPO)

A first-order policy gradient algorithm that uses a clipped surrogate objective to safely take the largest possible improvement step — without destabilizing training. Introduced by Schulman et al., OpenAI (2017).

1 · The Problem PPO Solves

🧩 Why Vanilla Policy Gradient Is Fragile

  • Standard policy gradient (REINFORCE, A2C) computes a gradient and takes a step: θ ← θ + α ∇J(θ).
  • If the step is too large, the new policy can deviate drastically from the old one — the advantage estimates become stale and training collapses.
  • If the step is too small, learning is painfully slow.
  • Choosing the right learning rate is brittle — it varies by environment and is hard to tune.
TRPO (Trust Region Policy Optimization) solved this by enforcing a hard KL-divergence constraint, but required expensive second-order optimization (conjugate gradients). PPO achieves similar safety with a simple first-order clipping trick.
2 · The Probability Ratio rt(θ)

📐 Measuring How Much the Policy Changed

  • PPO builds on the importance-weighted surrogate objective used in TRPO.
  • Instead of the raw log-probability gradient, we track the ratio of new-to-old action probabilities.
Probability Ratio
rt(θ) = πθ(at | st) / πθ_old(at | st)
rt(θ) = 1 → policy hasn't changed for this action
rt(θ) > 1 → new policy assigns higher probability to at than old
rt(θ) < 1 → new policy assigns lower probability to at than old
Unclipped Surrogate Objective (CPI — Conservative Policy Iteration)
LCPI(θ) = Et[ rt(θ) · Ât ]
Ât — advantage estimate at timestep t
Maximizing this encourages actions with positive advantage. But without constraints, rt(θ) can grow unboundedly large.
3 · The Clipped Surrogate Objective

✂️ PPO-Clip — The Core Idea

  • PPO-Clip adds a hard constraint on how far rt(θ) can deviate from 1 using a clip function.
  • This implicitly limits the policy update size without any KL penalty or second-order computation.
PPO-Clip Objective

ε — clip hyperparameter, typically 0.1 or 0.2
clip(r, 1−ε, 1+ε) — forces r to stay in [1−ε, 1+ε]

The min makes the objective a lower bound. When Ât > 0: we want r to grow but cap it at 1+ε. When Ât < 0: we want r to shrink but floor it at 1−ε. Either way, the policy can't move too far.
4 · Two PPO Variants

🔀 PPO-Clip vs PPO-KL Penalty

✂️ PPO-Clip (recommended)
L = E[min(r·Â, clip(r,1−ε,1+ε)·Â)]

No explicit KL term. Clipping acts as an implicit constraint. Simpler, more robust, and the default in practice. ε = 0.2 works across most tasks.

📏 PPO-KL Penalty
L = E[r·Â] − β · KL[π_old ‖ π_new]

Adds adaptive KL penalty coefficient β. β is increased if KL exceeds target dtarg, decreased if below. More principled but harder to tune.

In the original paper, PPO-Clip outperformed PPO-KL on most benchmarks. PPO-Clip is the de-facto standard when people say "PPO".
5 · Full PPO Loss Function

📉 Three-Term Combined Objective

  • In practice, the policy and value networks often share lower layers. The full loss combines three terms.
Combined PPO Objective
LPPO(θ) = LCLIP(θ) − c1 · LVF(θ) + c2 · S[πθ](st)
LCLIP(θ) — clipped policy gradient (maximize)
LVF(θ) = (Vθ(st) − Vtargt)² — value function MSE loss (minimize)
S[πθ](st) — entropy bonus: encourages exploration by rewarding policy spread
c1 — value loss coefficient (typically 0.5)
c2 — entropy coefficient (typically 0.01)
The entropy bonus prevents premature convergence to a deterministic policy. As training progresses, c2 is sometimes annealed toward 0.
6 · Advantage Estimation — GAE

⚡ Generalized Advantage Estimation

  • PPO uses GAE(γ, λ) to estimate advantages — a weighted sum of n-step TD residuals.
  • λ controls the bias-variance tradeoff: λ=0 is pure 1-step TD (low variance, biased), λ=1 is Monte Carlo (unbiased, high variance).
TD Residual
δt = rt + γ · V(st+1) − V(st)

Typical values: γ = 0.99, λ = 0.95
7 · The Epoch Loop — What Makes PPO Efficient

🔁 Multiple Update Epochs per Data Batch

  • A2C/REINFORCE discard collected transitions after a single gradient step.
  • PPO reuses the same batch of transitions for K epochs of minibatch SGD — dramatically improving sample efficiency.
  • The clipping ensures that even after multiple epochs on the same data, the policy doesn't stray too far from where the data was collected.

8 · Full PPO Algorithm

⚙️ Step-by-Step

  1. Initialize policy network πθ and value network Vφ (may share layers).
  2. For each iteration, run the current policy πθ_old in the environment for T timesteps across N parallel actors, collecting transitions (st, at, rt, st+1).
  3. Compute TD residuals δt and GAE advantage estimates Ât for all timesteps using the current value network.
  4. Compute value targets: Vtargt = Ât + Vφ(st).
  5. For K epochs, shuffle the N×T transitions and split into minibatches of size M.
  6. For each minibatch, compute the PPO loss LPPO(θ) and update θ via gradient ascent (Adam optimizer).
  7. Set θold ← θ. Repeat from step 2.

10 · Pros & Cons

⚖️ Strengths & Limitations

Strengths
  • Simple to implement (first-order only)
  • Stable training — clip prevents catastrophic updates
  • Works on both discrete & continuous actions
  • Reuses data for K epochs — more efficient than A2C
  • Strong default choice across many domains
  • Scales well with parallel actors
Limitations
  • On-policy — cannot reuse old replay data
  • Less sample-efficient than SAC/TD3 on continuous tasks
  • Sensitive to ε and K — too large causes instability
  • Clipping is a heuristic; no formal guarantee like TRPO
  • Still requires many environment interactions

13 · Extensions & Related Algorithms

🚀 Building on PPO

  • PPG (Phasic Policy Gradient) — separates policy and value optimization phases for better representations.
  • APPO / IMPALA — asynchronous distributed variants of PPO for large-scale training.
  • DAPO — decoupled clip ratios for actor and reference policies; used in LLM post-training.
  • GRPO — group relative policy optimization; removes the critic entirely using group-normalized advantages.
  • SAC — off-policy alternative for continuous control; often more sample-efficient than PPO.
